About

I am an architect and a data analyst with a specialisation in urban data and skills in GIS and spatial analysis. I have a background in architecture with over ten years of experience in the problematics of the city and in urban and architectural design.

I develop studies and applications aimed at improving the understanding of the urban systems. In particular, I work at bringing quantitative methods into the practice of architectural and urban design.

What's for lunch?

  • To the website: whatforlunch.duccioaiazzi.com
  • Date: June 2016

"What's for lunch?" is an exploration of food habits of Londoners, depending on their income and their area of residency. The rationale behind the project is that different social classes have different consumption patterns, hence demand different types of retail. We tried to catch these differences by looking at the name of these premises and the appearance of some specific words or tokens. Visit whatforlunch.duccioaiazzi.com to explore yourself how different premises are distributed across London and how they relate to the average income of the neighbourhood.
